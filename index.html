The moment

A turning point Mississippi cannot miss

Artificial intelligence is rewriting how economies compete and how talent decides where to build a life. Over the past decade, roughly 70,000 more people moved out of Mississippi than moved in, and close to half were young adults aged 25 to 34, many of them college educated and following opportunity to other states.

The Capital City Future Forum reframes that challenge as a choice we can win: prepare Mississippians to lead in an AI-driven economy, and give the next generation a reason to build that future at home.

Why Jackson

The hub of a statewide network

Jackson sits at the center of the South and at the center of Mississippi's university ecosystem. The capital and its metro are home to a concentration of talent few cities its size can claim.

Jackson State UniversityBelhaven UniversityMillsaps CollegeTougaloo CollegeMississippi CollegeHinds Community CollegeUMMC

That strength makes Jackson the natural hub of an AI-ready state: the place where research, training, and employers connect, then send opportunity outward along the spokes to every corner of Mississippi.

The Capital City Future Forum brings together Mississippi's students, educators, and industry leaders to explore how artificial intelligence is reshaping the classroom and the workplace, equipping the next generation with the skills, tools, and confidence to lead in an AI-driven economy.
